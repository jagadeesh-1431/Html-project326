Introduction

The Luna 25 mission, launched by Roscosmos, aims to renew lunar exploration efforts by landing near the Moon’s south pole. The mission’s goal is to conduct scientific investigations, explore potential resources, and set the stage for future lunar research.

Mission Objectives

Mapping Lunar Terrain

Detailed mapping of the lunar south pole region to identify topographic features and assess potential landing sites for future missions.

Geological Studies

Analysis of lunar soil samples to understand the Moon's composition and surface properties.

Water Ice Detection

Locating water ice deposits to support potential lunar habitats and future deep-space missions.

Temperature Measurements

Gathering data on lunar surface temperatures to study variations between day and night cycles in polar regions.

Technical Specifications

Launch Vehicle

Soyuz-2.1b with Fregat upper stage

Landing Zone

Near the lunar south pole, specifically the Boguslavsky Crater

Mission Duration

Approx. 1 year with extended mission potential

Primary Instruments

Gamma-ray and neutron spectrometer, thermal mapper, dust analyzer, and soil probe
